Learn More

Students need to know that what they are learning has meaning and usefulness. Robotics and coding projects and activities challenge students to apply the learned STEM knowledge and 21st-century skills to real-world scenarios.

The curriculum at is well designed that connects students to real world applications through hands-on activities and challenges, immersing them in STEM subjects such as variables and stability, systems thinking and modelling.

Today’s robotics and coding students will be tomorrow’s thought leaders, innovators, and creative problem solvers. They practice systems-thinking skills, not just in relation to robot parts and lines of code but in relation to other people as well.

Lines of codes might be the least important outcome of coding, just as an assembled bot is not the end of a robotics classroom activity. The more important output of coding and robotics are computational-thinking skills, whereby students learn how to logically break down a problem.
